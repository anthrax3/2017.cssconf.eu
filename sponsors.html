From the community, for the community

CSSconf EU is a not-for-profit conference dedicated to the creatives and engineers who build the world’s most engaging user interfaces. For the third year in a row, CSSconf EU will gather a diverse audience of more than 400 brilliant developers and designers who share a passion for the design language of the web: CSS.

The talks focus on the latest technologies, cutting edge techniques, and tools. CSSconf creates a platform for the young and evolving CSS community. It bridges the gap between designers and developers and connects experienced professionals with new talent.

Your Support Is Needed

CSSconf EU is organized entirely by a team of committed volunteers and every cent we raise goes towards making it the high-quality, diverse event it is. We are always excited to discuss sponsorship and media partner opportunities, please reach out to contact@cssconf.eu for details.

Why sponsor CSSconf EU?

International, Local, Collaborative

CSSconf EU is one of the main events of Web Tech Fest Berlin - a week full of conferences, workshops, and hackathons dedicated to web development and design. Internationally, we work closely together with our colleagues from CSSconf in Australia, Asia, and the US.

Reaching Creatives in the Community…

35k+ YouTube subscribers | 4.2k+ Twitter followers | 1k+ WWWTF attendees

…from 30+ Countries

Every year, CSSconf EU is attended by people from all corners of the world: Germany, US, UK, Sweden, Australia, Russia, New Zealand, Norway, Switzerland, Netherlands, Denmark, France, Poland, Iran, Spain, Portugal, Austria, Lebanon, India, Canada, Finland, Estonia, Kenya, Japan.

Safe, Diverse and Inclusive

CSSconf EU holds itself up to high standards of community and diversity. Our priority is to create a welcoming and safe space. We strive to bring diverse voices on stage and we provide a lot of information about the application process, additional blog posts covering the topic, and personal support (emails, slack chat) in order to achieve this goal. We also reach out via email to under-represented individuals and groups to encourage them to submit talk proposals. An example of the success of our efforts is that in 2013 and 2014, more than 40% of our speakers were female.

Educational and Encouraging

To achieve our goal we actively encourage new and upcoming speakers, we run free CSS workshops for beginners, we collaborate with tech education initiatives, and we enforce a Code of Conduct.

Enabling

We offer Diversity Tickets which enable more members of under-represented groups to attend CSSconf EU. Sponsors can help us with that, too – ask us for ways to get involved!

Strictly Not-for-profit

CSSconf EU is organized by volunteers. Every cent we raise goes towards making the conference better.
